# Product

## Register

brand

> **Note on split surfaces:** The public homepage is brand register (design IS the product — first impression for diners). The admin area is product register (design serves the owner — tool for managing the pizza catalog). Commands can override this default per surface.

## Users

**Public homepage:** Locals and tourists. Locals want quick info — hours, address, what's on the menu tonight. Tourists found the place through a recommendation or search and want to feel the atmosphere before committing to a table. Both groups are on their phones, probably standing outside or in a moment of indecision. Speed and clarity matter.

**Admin area:** The pizzeria owner. One person, occasional use — adding a pizza, changing a price, toggling availability. They're not a developer. The interface should feel like a well-organized piece of paper, not a CMS.

## Product Purpose

Mulino is a neighborhood pizzeria. The homepage exists so people can find the place, browse the menu, and decide to come in. It is not a delivery platform, not a reservation system, not a brand campaign — it is a door into the restaurant.

Success looks like: a new visitor lands on the homepage, sees something they want to eat, finds the address without thinking, and shows up.

## Brand Personality

Genuine, unhurried, rooted.

Voice: direct and warm. Doesn't try to impress. Describes the food as it is, not as a lifestyle proposition. No superlatives, no jargon. The copy sounds like the owner talking, not a copywriter.

## Anti-references

- **Generic Italian restaurant tropes**: red-and-green color pairings, checkered tablecloth motifs, pizza-slice icons, tourist-trap warmth. The identity comes from the specific place, not the category.
- **Bland local business template**: stock photography hero, three-column card grid, generic sans-serif on a light-gray background. This is the default anyone gets for $15/month from a website builder; it communicates nothing about Mulino specifically.

## Design Principles

1. **Locality as identity** — this is one specific place, not a brand that could be franchised. Every design decision should reinforce specificity over scalability.
2. **The food is the content** — the menu is the heart of the site. Everything else (hero, contacts) exists to frame it.
3. **Unhurried pacing** — the layout breathes. Space is not wasted, but it is not nervous either. Sections take their time.
4. **Honest hierarchy** — what a diner needs (menu, hours, address) should be reachable without reading instructions or squinting.
5. **Admin as tool** — the owner's time is valuable. The admin area is efficient, predictable, and out of the way; it does not try to be beautiful.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Focus on contrast (body text ≥4.5:1), keyboard navigation for all interactive elements, and meaningful alt text for any food photography. The contacts section must be screen-reader friendly (address as `<address>`, phone as a callable `<a href="tel:...">` link).
